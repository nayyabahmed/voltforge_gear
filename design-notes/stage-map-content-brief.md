# Stage Map - Content Brief for the Illustrated "Tech Hunt" Map

**Date:** 2026-07-19
**Purpose:** Everything a generative-AI illustrator (or human artist)
needs to produce a large, poster-style adventure map of the whole
handbook journey - in the style of a treasure-hunt / quest map, where
the reader travels through five territories from "curious beginner" to
"driving a buggy you designed yourself".

Use: paste the Base Prompt (bottom) into the image tool, then feed the
territory and waypoint lists as the map's content. Keep label text
EXACTLY as written here (British English, matches the book).

---

## The Big Picture

- **Journey:** one winding trail, start (bottom-left) to summit
  (top-right), through five distinct territories, one per Part.
- **Traveller:** an 11-year-old engineer with a notebook and a
  showcase shelf that fills with trophies (mini project artefacts)
  along the way.
- **Prize at the summit:** a fast 3D printed brushless RC buggy, built
  and understood by the traveller - flag planted: "I designed this."
- **Three promises to show on the trail** (the honest milestones):
  - Stage flag at Ch 32: "It rolls!" (pushed by hand)
  - Stage flag at Ch 33: "It drives itself!" (first motor)
  - Stage flag at Ch 34: "YOU drive it!" (radio control)
- **Tone:** adventurous, warm, a little whimsical; celebrates failure
  as learning ("wreckage read = treasure found"), never scary.

## Recurring Map Furniture

  trophy gets a tiny shelf slot; the shelf appears filled near the
  summit.
- **Safety gates** - goggle-arch motif at Ch 10 and Ch 22 (and small

  you build."
- **Engineering notebook** - the traveller carries it in every scene;
  it visibly thickens through the territories.
- **Wreckage-as-treasure** - small X-marks with broken paper bridges /
  snapped parts and a magnifying glass: "Read the failure - it's a
  clue."
- **The bath, the dominoes, the dartboard, the paperclip** - tiny
  Easter-egg cameos from famous chapter stories, scattered for readers
  to find (a treasure-hunt within the map).

  What happens? What leaves? What connects?" (systems thinking).

## Style Direction for the Illustrator / genAI

- Format: large landscape poster, 16:9 or A2/A1 print ratio; high
  detail that rewards close looking (Where's-Wally density in places).
- Style: hand-drawn adventure/treasure map - parchment or warm paper
  base, inked trail, watercolour territories; think children's atlas
  meets fantasy quest map, but the props are REAL engineering objects
  drawn accurately (calipers look like calipers, the ESC has three
  motor wires).
- Colour: each territory gets its own palette (woods greens ->
  workshop ambers -> canyon electric blues -> ascent greys/oranges ->
  summit golds); the trail is one continuous warm colour throughout.
- Lettering: friendly hand-lettered labels, exactly the label text
  given above; British English; no emoji on the map itself.
- People: the traveller can appear at several trail points (drawn
  generic and inclusive); an adult figure appears at the safety gates.
- Accuracy guardrails: buggy has four wheels, visible suspension and a
  rear-mounted motor; the printer is an FDM bed-slinger or CoreXY; no
  glowing sci-fi nonsense on the technical props.
