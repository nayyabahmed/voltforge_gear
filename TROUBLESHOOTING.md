# Troubleshooting Guide

Symptom -> likely causes -> fix -> the topic that explains why. This guide
grows during the build: every real problem we hit and solve becomes a row,
so the guide is made of true stories, not guesses.

Remember the method from Part 1: change one thing at a time, and ask "which
system has failed?" before replacing parts.

---

## 3D Printing

| Symptom | Likely causes | First fix | Topic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Print detaches mid-print ("spaghetti") | Poor first layer | Watch and re-do the first layer | 11 |
| Corners lift off the plate | Warping | Heated bed, no draughts, rounded corners | 11 |
| Hole too small for its shaft | Normal printing behaviour | Fit coupon, adjust hole size | 07, 11 |

## Maintenance Rules

1. Add a row when a problem is actually solved, with the real cause.
2. Keep one symptom per row; link the topic that teaches the concept.
3. If a symptom keeps recurring, consider whether the design (not the fix)
   needs to change - that is a Part 4 design review topic.
